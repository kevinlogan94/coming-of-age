# Character Bible

Recap of the character-building conversation for the pre-college narrative game.

## Setting

The story takes place during the final week before John (also called Kevin interchangeably) leaves home for college, set about an hour and a half away from his hometown.

## Core Cast

### John

The protagonist, navigating relationships, friendships, and family expectations during his last week home.

### Rachel

John's on-and-off girlfriend for the past two years, since junior year. They met in high school biology class, sitting next to each other, and became best friends before becoming a couple—notably, John was still dating Allison when he met Rachel. Rachel is staying in the hometown for college rather than moving with John. She has a history of depression and a pattern of cheating; the plan is that after the game's events, she cheats on John again and he eventually breaks up with her. She later ends up with an old mutual friend.

### Allison

A flute player John dated in marching band, in between his relationships with Rachel. When John met Allison, she was already in a relationship but left her boyfriend for John. Several months before the game begins, John broke up with Allison in favor of Rachel. John and Allison never get back together; she eventually moves on with someone else, an old mutual friend of theirs.

### Kyle

John's close friend since middle school, primarily bonded through gaming. He's a single, spoiled only child. He's attending the same college as John but they won't be roommates—he's moving in with Elliot instead.

### Elliot

Another middle-school friend and one of John's best friends alongside Kyle. He's extremely intelligent, a bit quiet, loves games, and is deeply supportive.

## Family

### Steve (Dad)

Focused on making sure John is prepared for the real world; represents practical expectation and pressure.

### Lisa (Mom)

Loves John deeply and wants to support him; represents emotional safety and attachment.

### Elizabeth (Little Sister)

Still lives at home; represents legacy and being watched/admired by a younger sibling.

### Wes (Big Brother / Mentor)

Attended the same college John is about to attend but transferred away. He left not out of failure, but because he wanted better mentorship/teaching to advance his career, even though he loved the social side of that school. He visits home occasionally and gives John advice, though it comes from a place of intentional, strategic thinking rather than pure comfort.

### Ace

John's Shih Tzu, serving as an emotional anchor and quiet, grounding presence throughout the week.

## Design Themes

- Rachel and Allison both represent contrasting "what ifs"—one is emotional comfort with instability, the other is clarity and a missed opportunity.
- Kyle and Elliot represent familiar identity vs. thoughtful growth.
- The family unit (Dad / Mom / Sister / Wes) creates a spectrum from "stay safe" to "strategically pivot," with Wes uniquely representing the idea that leaving something isn't always failure.
- Naming inconsistency (John / Kevin) still needs resolving.
- Heavy overlapping relationship drama should stay grounded in character psychology rather than escalate purely for plot drama.
