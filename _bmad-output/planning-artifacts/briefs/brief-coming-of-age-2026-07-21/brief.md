# Game Brief: Last Week Home

## Vision

*Last Week Home* is a short 2D top-down story game about Kevin’s final days in Northern Kentucky before college in 2012. Built in Phaser for a web/itch-first release, it asks the player to spend one irreplaceable week being present — walking familiar places, reading the room through texts and limited time, and sitting face-to-face with the people who made him.

**Core fantasy:** Live one last ordinary week in the place that made you — its streets, habits, and people — and practice leaving well before college takes you.

**Emotional promise:** The player walks away feeling *this week mattered because it won’t stay — and I was there for it.* Not “I won the romance.”

**Brand image (trailer / store face):** Nick’s car drop-off — he walks away assuming continuity; Kevin already knows the chapter is ending.

**Playable emotional climax:** Rachel — asymmetric farewell (commitment poured out vs. indifference). Relationship outcomes help drive endings. Betrayal/aftermath may color endings and epilogue — it is not the poster promise.

**Core message:** Everything changes — so be present with the people who love you, and make time for what matters. When life calls you forward, go. Give the world your presence.

The project is an entry release — solo-led, LPC + custom/AI portraits, story and music help from trusted people — sized to prove Phaser and earn traction without a giant investment. Specificity of place, memory, and farewell structure is the product.

## Scope & MVP

**Team:** Solo-led by Kevin (design/dev/Phaser). Story help from Elliot. Writing help planned. Music help from brother (likely). No dedicated full-time art hire — LPC + generated/custom portraits for gaps.

**Engine / platforms:** Phaser. **Ship target:** web build on itch.io first (shareable link). Steam wrapper is a later upside, not a day-one requirement.

**MVP hypothesis:** Can a short, choice-driven farewell week — portraits, texts, limited who-you-see, three endings — deliver a complete emotional experience people will finish and share?

**MVP boundary (shippable first release):**
- One compressed “final week” ≈ **8 time blocks**
- Load-bearing cast: **Kevin, Steve, Lisa (Mom), Rachel, Nick**
- Verbs: walk/run, talk+portraits, text+scheduling/ditch, examine
- **Three endings** driven by Nick / Rachel / Family (Steve + Mom) presence flags
- Content pool sized so one playthrough ≈ **2–3 hours** (player sees a path through the pool; authored scenes denser than one run)
- **Four long set-pieces:** Nick car farewell; Rachel asymmetric goodbye; Steve in-week confrontation (fear/discipline — not the later homesick texts); Mom attachment/goodbye
- Locations: small set (home, Nick drop-off route, Rachel-related place, one shared hangout)
- Art: Liberated Pixel Cup (+ extensions) for world/sprites; curated custom or AI-assisted portraits for the load-bearing cast; clean attribution
- Audio: brother-led or original melancholic underscore; light SFX

**Content budget:** ~4 long set-pieces; ~6–8 medium scenes; ~6–10 short connective beats; explore/examine glue; opening + epilogue. Single run uses ~60–70% of the optional pool via scheduling. If content slips, **cut playtime promise** before adding filler systems.

**Out of MVP:** Allison romance branch; Kyle/Elliot quests; jigsaw; stealth; Steam-critical features; four+ endings; large open town.

## Core Fundamentals

**Genre:** Short 2D top-down narrative adventure / coming-of-age story game with light social-scheduling choices.

**Playtime:** About 2–3 hours per playthrough.

**Core loop:** Enter a time block → explore a small set of places → examine for nostalgia / receive texts → choose who to show up for (talk + portrait dialogue + meaningful choices) → major scene resolves → time advances → repeat across the final week → finale and one of three endings.

**Time model:** Calendar advances after major scenes (Persona / *Life is Strange* cadence), not free-roam of the entire week at once and not sleep-only.

**Gameplay pillars:**
1. **Presence over plot** — Power comes from showing up and saying the hard thing; twist machinery is not the engine.
2. **Faces in conversation** — LPC-style top-down bodies in the world; higher-res portraits when people talk. Intimacy is a UI promise.
3. **Choices that leave a mark** — Limited social time, competing invites, and dialogue forks feed relationship/presence flags → three endings.

**Primary verbs (MVP):** Walk / run; talk (portrait dialogue + choices); text (including competing hangout invites); limited “who you see” per time block; examine (look / read / nostalgia — not puzzle mini-games).

**Social-consequence pattern:** Commit to a hangout → a second invite can arrive → accepting can mean ditching the first person → presence/relationship flags shift. Teaches *you make time for what matters* without a sermon.

**Choice → endings:** Track presence/honesty with **Nick**, **Rachel**, and **Family (Steve + Mom)**. Three endings reflect how Kevin leaves each thread — including space for a cleaner break with Rachel than real life, framed as honesty/presence rather than a moral high score.

**Out of MVP verb kit:** Jigsaw/puzzle genre; stealth genre (any “almost got caught” memory is scripted story only, if used at all).

## Audience, Market & Differentiation

**Primary audience:** Players roughly 18–35 who want short, emotionally direct narrative games (~2–3 hours). Time-poor but story-hungry; comfortable with choice-driven dialogue; discover via shareable web/itch links, trailers, and word of mouth.

**Secondary audience:** Players (and some non-habitual gamers) drawn by Midwest / leaving-home nostalgia more than by genre fandom.

**Not the audience:** Systems-first RPG players; 20h+ campaign hunters; anyone sold a betrayal-mystery or supernatural small-town thriller.

**Market posture:** Traction-friendly indie narrative lane. Price intuition if/when paid: roughly **$4.99–$9.99** (itch free-or-PWYW possible first). Opportunity is cultural specificity + the hook “one last week home,” not production arms-race.

**Comparable titles — take vs. leave:**

| Title | Take | Leave |
|---|---|---|
| *Life is Strange* | Choice weight; time advancing after scenes; small-town teenage ache | Rewind powers; mystery conspiracy; long episode structure |
| *Persona 5* | Limited social time; who you see matters; competing priorities | Dungeons; combat; stylish anime JRPG scale; months-long calendar |
| *Stardew Valley* | Top-down daily life; portrait-on-talk intimacy; place attachment | Farming loop; endless seasons; marriage/meta progression |
| *The Last of Us* | Emotional seriousness; relationships as the point; quiet between storms | AAA action; gore; long cinematic campaign |

**Differentiator:** A specific **Northern Kentucky, 2012** farewell week with autobiographical texture — LPC bodies, custom dialogue portraits, text-driven scheduling (including ditching), and a brand image built on Nick’s **mismatched friend goodbye**, not a supernatural mystery or romance-betrayal poster. Edge is specificity and honesty of execution, not a technical moat.

## Content & Direction

**Setting:** Northern Kentucky, **2012** texture — period phones/texts, local places, high-school aftermath (drumline identity as examine/backstory flavor, not a game mode). College is ~1.5 hours away; the week is the threshold.

**Narrative approach:** Autofiction-flavored grounded realism. No supernatural framing. Story through conversations, texts, and what you miss when you choose someone else. Steve’s in-week weight is fear dressed as discipline; Mom is attachment/emotional safety. The mutual homesick apology with **Steve** happens **after** Kevin has left — epilogue/ending color only.

**Cast:** Playable weight — Steve, Mom, Rachel, Nick. Texture-only unless earned later — Elizabeth, Wes, Ace. Dropped from playable — Allison, Kyle, Elliot.

**Art:** 2D top-down LPC in-world; higher-res portraits on dialogue. AI gap assets allowed if curated for consistency (faces, lighting, 2012 wardrobe). Nostalgia via examineables and place dressing.

**Audio:** Warm, slightly melancholic underscore; silence in hard conversations; simple UI/text cues.

**Replayability:** Light — alternate priorities and endings. Not a content treadmill.

## Risks & Open Questions

**Risks**
- **Content volume vs. 2–3h promise** — Mitigation: lock the 8-block / 4 set-piece budget; cut length before adding systems.
- **AI + LPC visual mismatch** — Mitigation: portrait style sheet; human pick/reject; fewer faces done well.
- **LPC licensing/attribution** — Mitigation: credit sheet early (many assets CC-BY-SA).
- **Autobiographical exposure** — Mitigation: store promise = last week home / Nick image; endings handle aftermath with care; no unconfirmed cheating as fact.
- **Phaser narrative tooling** — Mitigation: JSON or Ink/Yarn-style pipeline early; prototype text+time-advance before map sprawl.
- **Crowded short-narrative market** — Mitigation: NKY 2012 specificity + ditching/texts + Nick brand image.

**Open questions (for GDD)**
- Exact ending definitions (what each of the three endings shows)
- Concrete location list and 2012 nostalgia reference pack
- Portrait production pipeline (Gemini prompts vs. hand touch-up standards)
- How hard ditching hits mechanically (temporary vs. ending-critical)
- Whether Ace / Elizabeth / Wes earn a single scene post-MVP
- Paid price vs. free/PWYW itch launch experiment
